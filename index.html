About Us

Economic Environment

- Malaysia has a diversified economy, with key sectors including manufacturing, services, and agriculture.
- It boasts free trade agreements (FTAs), making it attractive for global business.
- The country’s GDP has grown steadily, supported by strong export activities in electronics, palm oil, and petroleum products.

Financial Environment

- The Malaysian Ringgit (MYR) is the local currency, with financial institutions offering trade financing services.
- The Central Bank of Malaysia (Bank Negara Malaysia) ensures financial stability.

Socio-Cultural Environment

- Malaysia is a multicultural society, with Malay, Chinese, and Indian influences, making cultural sensitivity key in trade negotiations.
- Bahasa Malaysia is the official language, but English is widely used in business.

Political-Legal Environment

- Malaysia follows common law principles with stable governance.
- Trade is regulated by licensing, tariffs, and customs duties, with open trade policies as part of ASEAN and global alliances.

Airports

In total, Malaysia has 63 airports (39 in East Malaysia and 24 in Peninsular Malaysia). Among them, 38 airports have scheduled passenger service on commercial airlines. Other than that, Malaysia has 7 international airports.

Seaports

Malaysia's strategic location along major trade routes makes it a key hub for global maritime trade and transshipment in Southeast Asia. It ranks fifth globally in shipping connectivity, outperforming developed nations like Germany and the Netherlands. As the world's largest exporter of LNG and a leading palm oil producer, Malaysia boasts major port facilities, including the world’s biggest palm oil terminal. Its growing infrastructure, affordable land, and technological advancements strengthen its position in the shipping industry. With these advantages, Malaysia could surpass Singapore in handling Asia’s maritime cargo traffic in the future.
